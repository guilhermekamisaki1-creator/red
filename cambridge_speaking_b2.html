Resumo Rápido

O que mais cai no Speaking

Antes de qualquer coisa: entenda a prova. O Speaking do FCE tem 4 partes, dura ~14 min e é avaliado por 4 critérios. Aqui está o mapa.

25%Gramática & Vocabulário
25%Discurso (coerência & coesão)
25%Pronúncia
25%Interação Comunicativa
ℹ️ O que isso significa na prática

Gramática perfeita = 25% da nota. Você pode errar gramática e ainda passar — mas não consegue passar sem falar de forma coerente. Conectivos, desenvolvimento de ideias e interação com o parceiro são tão importantes quanto estrutura.

Parte 1

Interview

⏱ ~2 minutos

Perguntas sobre você: rotina, gostos, experiências, planos. Falar com o examiner, não com o parceiro.

Parte 2

Long Turn

⏱ ~4 minutos (1 min cada)

Você recebe 2 fotos, fala por ~1 min comparando-as e responde a uma pergunta. Depois o parceiro faz o mesmo e você comenta brevemente.

Parte 3

Collaborative Task

⏱ ~3 minutos

Você e seu parceiro discutem opções juntos, negociam e chegam a uma decisão. Interação é essencial aqui.

Parte 4

Discussion

⏱ ~5 minutos

O examiner faz perguntas mais profundas sobre o tema da Parte 3. Você deve expressar opiniões, justificar e discutir.

Seção 1

Estratégias de Speaking

Técnicas concretas para falar com fluência, sem travar e soar natural — não "decorado".

🕐 Como ganhar tempo sem parecer inseguro

Pausas longas em silêncio = ruim. Pausas preenchidas = natural. Use estas frases enquanto organiza o pensamento:

"That's a really interesting question…"
Quando precisar de 2–3 segundos para pensar
"Let me think about that for a moment…"
Tom pensativo, nunca parece inseguro
"Well, I'd say that…"
Abre qualquer resposta de forma natural
"It's hard to say exactly, but…"
Para perguntas difíceis — e já começa a responder
"To be honest, I've never really thought about that, but…"
Honesto e natural — compra 3+ segundos
"Now, that's something I feel quite strongly about…"
Para tópicos de opinião — soa apaixonado e fluente

🔗 Conectivos para soar B2+

Trocar "and" e "but" por conectivos variados é um dos atalhos mais rápidos para subir a nota. Use estes:

FunçãoConectivos B2+Exemplo em fala
Adicionar What's more · On top of that · Furthermore · Not only that, but "It's quick. What's more, it's very affordable."
Contrastar However · Having said that · On the other hand · That said · Even so "I love travelling. Having said that, it can be exhausting."
Causa / Razão Due to · Owing to · As a result · Given that · Seeing as "Given that it was late, we decided to stay."
Exemplo For instance · For example · Take… for example · Such as "Take social media, for example — it changes everything."
Concluir All in all · On balance · When all's said and done · Ultimately "All in all, I think it's a positive development."
Especular It seems to me that · I would imagine · I'd assume · It's likely that "It seems to me that these people are colleagues."

📈 Como desenvolver respostas sem travar

💡 Fórmula P-E-E (Point · Evidence · Expand)

Para qualquer pergunta de opinião:

  1. Point — dê sua opinião/ponto principal (1 frase)
  2. Evidence — justifique com exemplo, razão ou experiência pessoal (1–2 frases)
  3. Expand — adicione nuance, contraste ou especulação (1–2 frases)
Exemplo — "Do you enjoy travelling?"
[Point] "Absolutely — travelling is something I'm really passionate about. [Evidence] I've been to several countries and every trip has taught me something new, both about different cultures and about myself. [Expand] Having said that, I do think there's something to be said for staying home and appreciating your own surroundings — you don't always need to go far to discover something meaningful."

💬 Frases para cada parte da prova

Parte 1 — Perguntas pessoais. Responda com 3–5 frases, não com 1 só.

"I'd describe myself as someone who…"
Para falar sobre personalidade/hábitos
"As far as I'm concerned…"
Para dar opinião pessoal forte
"I've been doing… for about…"
Present Perfect Continuous — mostra domínio de tense
"I tend to… rather than…"
Expressa preferência com sofisticação
"In my experience…"
Ancora resposta em experiência real
"I suppose what I enjoy most is…"
Tom reflexivo, soa natural

Parte 2 — Comparar 2 fotos + responder pergunta. Nunca descreva apenas — compare e especule.

"In the first photo, I can see what appears to be…"
Abertura para descrever com cautela
"…whereas in the second photo…"
Conectivo de contraste essencial na Parte 2
"The most striking difference between the two is…"
Frase de comparação direta — soa B2+
"Both photos seem to suggest…"
Para identificar tema comum
"The person in the photo looks as if they might be…"
Especulação — essencial na Parte 2
"I'd imagine this was taken in a… setting because…"
Justifica especulação — mostra raciocínio
"If I had to choose, I'd say the first photo is more…"
Para responder a pergunta da tarefa
"It's hard to say for certain, but…"
Nunca afirme algo que não pode saber

Parte 3 — Discussão colaborativa. Você deve interagir, não monologar. Convide o parceiro a falar.

"What do you think about this option?"
Convida parceiro — essencial para nota de interação
"I see what you mean, but I'm not entirely sure that…"
Discorda sem atacar — tom profissional
"That's a valid point. Having said that…"
Concorda parcialmente e desenvolve
"Shall we move on to the next one?"
Gerencia o tempo — mostra liderança natural
"I think we can both agree that…"
Para convergir na decisão final
"Could you elaborate on that?"
Pede que parceiro desenvolva — boa interação

Parte 4 — Discussão com o examiner. Respostas mais longas, opiniões justificadas, pensamento crítico.

"I strongly believe that… because…"
Opinião firme + justificativa
"There are several factors to consider here…"
Sinaliza que vai desenvolver bem
"On the one hand… on the other hand…"
Estrutura clássica de argumento balanceado
"I think it depends largely on…"
Resposta nuançada — evita simplismo
"In my view, the most significant aspect is…"
Prioriza argumento — soa analítico
"Society as a whole would benefit from…"
Escala o argumento — pensamento macro
⚠️ NÃO faça isso no Speaking
  • Não fique em silêncio. Use frases de ganho de tempo — qualquer coisa é melhor que silêncio.
  • Não responda com 1 frase. "Yes, I like music" = nota baixa. Desenvolva sempre.
  • Não ignore o parceiro na Parte 3. Interagir é 25% da nota.
  • Não descreva apenas as fotos. Na Parte 2, você deve comparar e especular.
  • Não use o mesmo vocabulário. Repita "nice" 5 vezes = nota baixa em lexical range.

Seção 3

Gramática que impressiona no oral

Do seu material FCE — as estruturas mais úteis para speaking. Você não precisa de gramática perfeita; precisa de gramática variada.

⏳ Tempos Verbais — use variedade

No speaking B2+, o examiner quer ouvir mais de um tempo verbal na mesma resposta. Compare:

❌ Nível A2 — só Present Simple

"I like music. I listen to music every day. I enjoy all genres."

vs
✓ Nível B2+ — vários tempos

"I've been into music since I was a kid — I used to play guitar, and I've been taking piano lessons for about a year now."

Tempo verbalQuando usar no SpeakingFrase de exemplo
Present Perfect Continuous
have been + -ing		 Ação que durou e ainda tem efeito agora — ótimo para Part 1 "I've been learning English for about 10 years."
Past Perfect
had + participle		 Sequência de eventos passados — mostra sophistication "By the time I arrived, everyone had already left."
used to / would Hábitos passados — muito natural no Speaking "I used to hate sport, but now I love it."
Conditional (I'd / It would) Especulação sobre fotos (Parte 2) e opiniões (Parte 4) "I'd imagine this is a rather stressful job."
Passive voice Discussões sobre sociedade, tecnologia, ambiente "A lot of pressure is placed on young people today."

🔑 Verb Patterns — evite erros clássicos

Do seu material — os verbos que mais confundem e mais caem. Use no Speaking para mostrar precisão gramatical.

remember TO do

Lembrar de fazer algo (ainda não fez)

"Remember to call me later."
vs
remember -ING

Ter memória de algo já feito

"I remember meeting her for the first time."
✓ Use no Speaking

"I'll never forget visiting Paris for the first time — it was absolutely breathtaking."

stop TO do

Parar algo PARA fazer outra coisa

"We stopped to take photos of the view."
vs
stop -ING

Parar de fazer algo por completo

"I stopped eating meat two years ago."
⚠️ would NÃO funciona com estados

Estados: have, be, know, like, own, live (como estado)…

✗ Errado
I would have a dog when I was young.
✓ Correto
I used to have a dog when I was young.
✓ would — ação repetida (correto)
"Every summer, my family would rent a house by the sea and we'd spend the whole day on the beach."

Para especular sobre fotos ou situações hipotéticas:

ModalGrau de certezaExemplo no Speaking
must be~95% certo (positivo)"She must be a professional athlete."
can't be~95% certo (negativo)"It can't be cheap to live there."
could / might be~50% possível"This could be a hospital or a school."
must have beencerteza sobre passado"It must have been very challenging."
should havecrítica / arrependimento"They should have invested in renewables earlier."

🔄 Estruturas para soar mais fluente

"What strikes me most about this is…"
Estrutura de ênfase — soa analítico
"The thing about [X] is that…"
Cleft sentence — soa muito natural em fala
"What I find particularly interesting is…"
Estrutura de focalização — B2+ imediato
"It's not so much X as Y"
Contraste sofisticado — muito eficaz
"Not only is it… but it also…"
Inversão — advanced structure
"The more I think about it, the more I believe…"

Seção 4

Respostas Modelo B2+

Respostas anotadas para cada parte da prova. Analise a estrutura, não decore o conteúdo.

Q: "What kind of activities do you enjoy in your free time?"

✓ Resposta Modelo B2+ Parte 1 — Interview

"Well, I'd say I'm someone who really values their free time. I've been getting into photography lately — I started about a year ago and I've been making steady progress. What I particularly enjoy is the challenge of capturing the right moment. Having said that, I also used to play football when I was younger, so sport has always been part of my life. All in all, I think it's important to have a variety of interests — it keeps life interesting."

🔵 Present Perfect Continuous 🔴 Collocation ⚡ Cleft structure 🔗 Connectives × 2 ⏳ used to (past habit)

Q: "Do you think social media has a positive or negative effect on young people?"

✓ Resposta Modelo B2+ Parte 1 — Opinion question

"It's hard to give a definitive answer because I think it depends on how it's used. On the one hand, social media allows young people to connect, to express themselves and even take part in important conversations about society. On the other hand, there's growing evidence that excessive use can be detrimental to mental health. What I believe is that digital literacy — knowing how to use these platforms responsibly — should be taught in schools."

⚖️ Balanced argument structure 🔴 Collocation: take part in 📊 Passive voice 💡 Cleft: "What I believe"

Task: "Compare these two photos of people working, and say which job looks more rewarding."

✓ Resposta Modelo B2+ Parte 2 — Long Turn (1 minuto)

"In the first photo, I can see what appears to be a doctor or medical professional treating a patient in a clinical setting. The atmosphere looks quite intense and focused. Whereas in the second photo, there's someone who seems to be working outdoors — perhaps a landscape gardener or an architect on a building site."

"The most striking difference between the two is the environment — one is very controlled and sterile, the other open and physical. Both photos suggest a high level of dedication and expertise, though in very different ways."

"If I had to say which looks more rewarding, I'd choose the first — mainly because the direct impact on someone's health and wellbeing must be incredibly fulfilling. Having said that, working outdoors has its own obvious advantages."

🔭 Speculation: "appears to be" ⚖️ Contrast: "whereas" 📊 Modal: "must be" 🎯 Answers task directly

Task: "Discuss which of these activities would be best for a team-building event at work."

✓ Resposta Modelo B2+ Parte 3 — Collaborative discussion

[Você abre] "So, shall we start with the cooking class? I think it could work well because it encourages people to work together and make decisions as a group. What do you think?"

[Respondendo ao parceiro] "That's a valid point — an outdoor challenge would definitely build trust. I see what you mean, but I wonder if it might exclude people who aren't very sporty. Having said that, it does seem more memorable."

[Chegando à decisão] "I think we can both agree that the most important factor is inclusivity. On balance, the cooking class seems the safest option — but the escape room could also be worth considering. Shall we go with the cooking class, then?"

🤝 Invites partner × 2 ⚖️ Agrees + disagrees ✅ Reaches decision 🎯 Manages conversation

Q: "Do you think governments should do more to protect the environment?"

✓ Resposta Modelo B2+ Parte 4 — Discussion

"Absolutely, and I feel quite strongly about this. There are several factors to consider. Firstly, climate change is no longer a distant threat — it's something that's already having a significant impact on communities worldwide."

"On the one hand, governments have the power to enact legislation and make a real difference at scale — something individuals simply cannot do on their own. On the other hand, there's a risk of economic disruption if changes happen too rapidly."

"Ultimately, I believe that a balanced approach is needed — one that takes into account both environmental urgency and economic reality. The more we delay, the more costly the solutions will become."

💪 Strong opinion opener 📋 "Several factors" — signals development 🔴 Collocations × 3 🔄 Double comparative ✅ Reaches conclusion
Seção 5 — Simulado Interativo

Mini Simulado Cambridge Speaking

Perguntas no estilo real do FCE. Pressione START, fale em voz alta por 60 segundos, depois veja a resposta modelo. Treine como se fosse amanhã — porque é.

💡 Como usar este simulado
  1. Leia a pergunta em voz alta.
  2. Pressione START e responda por 60 segundos (fale de verdade).
  3. Quando o timer acabar, pressione Ver Resposta Modelo.
  4. Compare a sua com a modelo e identifique diferenças.

0 / 8 perguntas

Parte 1 — Interview

🎙️ Parte 1 — Pergunta 1 1:00
"Tell me about something you've been learning or doing recently that you find interesting."
Responda por 3–5 frases. Use Present Perfect Continuous. Desenvolva com exemplos.
✓ RESPOSTA MODELO B2+
"Well, I've been getting into photography over the past few months, which I've found incredibly rewarding. What I particularly enjoy is the challenge of capturing a fleeting moment — you can't make the same mistake twice, so to speak. I've been making steady progress, and I've even started taking an online course. Having said that, I still have a lot to learn, which is part of what makes it exciting."
🔑 O que fez bem: Usou Present Perfect Continuous, collocations, uma cleft sentence, conectivo de contraste. Duração ideal: 45–60 segundos.
🎙️ Parte 1 — Pergunta 2 1:00
"How have your habits or interests changed since you were younger?"
Use used to / would para hábitos passados. Contraste com o presente.
✓ RESPOSTA MODELO B2+
"Quite a lot, actually. When I was younger, I used to spend most of my free time playing video games — my friends and I would meet every weekend and stay up all night. Nowadays, I tend to take an interest in things that are a bit more — I suppose — meaningful. I've been reading more, and I've been trying to learn a new language. I think what changed was going to university — it broadened my perspective considerably."
🔑 Estruturas usadas: used to, would, Present Perfect Continuous, cleft sentence. Contraste passado/presente claro.

Parte 2 — Long Turn

🎙️ Parte 2 — Long Turn (1 min) 1:00
Compare these two situations: [Foto 1] A person studying alone late at night. [Foto 2] A group of students studying together in a library. Which situation do you think is more effective for learning?
Compare as duas fotos, especule sobre o contexto e responda a pergunta ao final. Use "whereas", "seems to", "might be".
✓ RESPOSTA MODELO B2+
"In the first photo, there's someone who appears to be studying alone late at night — they look quite focused, but also perhaps a little tired or stressed. Whereas in the second, a group of students seem to be collaborating in a library, which suggests a more social approach to learning. The most striking difference is the atmosphere — one is isolated, the other communal. Both could be effective depending on the person and the subject. If I had to choose, I'd say group study is often more motivating, particularly when tackling complex topics — though some subjects genuinely require quiet, focused, individual work."
🔑 Usa: appears to be, whereas, seems to, could be, if I had to choose — todas as estruturas-chave da Parte 2.
🎙️ Parte 2 — Short response (20 sec) 0:20
"Which situation do YOU prefer — studying alone or with others? Why?" (pergunta rápida ao parceiro)
Responda em 15–20 segundos. Curto, direto, mas com justificativa.
✓ RESPOSTA MODELO B2+
"Personally, I tend to prefer studying alone — I find I make more progress when I can focus without distractions. Having said that, group study can be invaluable for discussing ideas."
🔑 20 segundos é suficiente — não tente dizer tudo. Seja direto, use um conectivo e pare.

Parte 3 — Collaborative Task

🎙️ Parte 3 — Discussão colaborativa 1:00
A school wants to improve students' wellbeing. Discuss: [1] free sports facilities [2] mental health workshops [3] flexible timetables [4] a student wellbeing app. Which two would be most effective?
Imagine que você está discutindo com um parceiro. Proponha, reaja, negocie. Use frases de interação.
✓ RESPOSTA MODELO B2+
"So, shall we start with mental health workshops? I think they address the issue most directly — mental health is something that affects students' performance significantly. What do you reckon? … That's a fair point about the sports facilities — exercise does make a real difference to wellbeing. I see where you're coming from, but I wonder if the app might be too reliant on technology. On balance, I'd go for mental health workshops and flexible timetables — they seem the most sustainable in the long run."
🔑 Chave: Convide o parceiro. Use frases de concordância/discordância. Chegue a uma decisão clara.

Parte 4 — Discussion

🎙️ Parte 4 — Pergunta 1 1:00
"Some people believe that social media does more harm than good to young people's mental health. Do you agree?"
Resposta mais longa (45–60 seg). Estruture: opinião + dois argumentos + conclusão.
✓ RESPOSTA MODELO B2+
"I think there's a great deal of truth in that statement, but it's perhaps an oversimplification. On the one hand, there's compelling evidence that constant comparison on platforms like Instagram can have a detrimental effect on self-esteem, particularly among teenagers. On the other hand, social media can be a powerful tool for community-building and support — many young people find genuine connection online. What I believe is that the issue isn't social media itself, but the lack of digital literacy in schools. Ultimately, it's not so much about banning these platforms as teaching young people to use them responsibly."
🔑 Estruturas B2+: "it's perhaps an oversimplification" · "On the one hand / other" · cleft "What I believe" · "not so much X as Y"
🎙️ Parte 4 — Pergunta 2 1:00
"How important is it for schools to teach students about environmental issues?"
Use vocabulário de meio ambiente. Expresse opinião forte com justificativa.
✓ RESPOSTA MODELO B2+
"I feel very strongly that environmental education is absolutely essential — perhaps more so now than ever before. Given that climate change is one of the most pressing challenges of our time, schools have a responsibility to equip students with the knowledge to make informed decisions. What's more, young people are the ones who will be most affected — so they have the most at stake. Having said that, education alone isn't enough — it needs to be accompanied by systemic change at a government level. All in all, I believe it's not so much an option as a necessity."
🔑 Vocabulário: pressing challenges · equip students · informed decisions. Estruturas: given that · what's more · not so much X as Y.
🎙️ Parte 4 — Pergunta difícil 1:00
"Some people argue that working from home makes people less productive. What are your thoughts?"
Pergunta controversa — mostre que consegue ver os dois lados antes de concluir.
✓ RESPOSTA MODELO B2+
"That's an interesting claim, and I think it depends largely on the individual and the type of work involved. In my experience, remote working can actually boost productivity — you eliminate the lengthy commute, reduce office distractions and gain more control over your environment. On the other hand, some people genuinely struggle to maintain boundaries between work and personal life, which can be detrimental in the long run. I'd argue that the key is flexibility — a hybrid model gives employees the best of both worlds."
🔑 Boa resposta porque: não concorda nem discorda diretamente → mostra nuance → usa "I'd argue that" para posição pessoal no final.
Seção 6

Erros que você deve evitar

Lista curta e direta — os erros mais comuns de candidatos B2 que perdem nota à toa. Leia antes de dormir.

  • 1
    Resposta curta demais ❌ "Yes, I like music." → ✓ "Yes, music has always been a big part of my life. I've been listening to jazz lately, which I find incredibly relaxing…"
  • 2
    Só descrever as fotos na Parte 2 (sem comparar) ❌ "In the first photo there is a man. In the second photo there is a woman." → ✓ "…whereas in the second photo, the atmosphere seems much more relaxed, which suggests a very different working environment."
  • 3
    would com verbos de estado (erro clássico do seu material) ❌ "My family would have a small house." / "He would be shy." → ✓ "My family used to have a small house." / "He used to be shy."
  • 4
    Não convidar o parceiro na Parte 3 ❌ Falar 3 minutos sem parar, ignorando o parceiro completamente. → ✓ "What do you think?" / "Do you agree?" / "Shall we move on?"
  • 5
    Silêncio longo quando não sabe o que dizer ❌ [5 segundos de silêncio] → ✓ "That's a really interesting question — let me think about that for a moment…"
  • 6
    Vocabulário repetitivo (good, nice, big, bad) ❌ "It's very good and very nice. It was a very big change." → ✓ "It's remarkably beneficial. It was a substantial shift."
  • 7
    Dar opinião sem justificar ❌ "I agree with this. It is true." → ✓ "I agree, mainly because… Furthermore, research suggests that…"
  • 8
    Usar só um tempo verbal em toda a resposta ❌ "I work. I like work. I go to work every day." → ✓ Mix: "I've been working here for two years. I used to work remotely, but now I prefer the office."
  • 9
    Falar muito rápido / atropelar as palavras ❌ Velocidade = clareza? Não. Pronúncia clara vale 25% da nota. → ✓ Fale em ritmo natural — uma pausa bem colocada soa mais fluente do que pressa nervosa.
  • 10
